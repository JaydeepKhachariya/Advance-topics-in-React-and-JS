Closure

Imagine your friend gives you a backpack with some snacks. You can carry this backpack around, and whenever you're hungry, you just reach in and grab a snack. But here's the magic: even if your friend isn't around anymore, the snacks in the backpack are still there for you to enjoy! In JavaScript, a closure is like that backpack. It's when a function remembers and can access the variables from the place where it was created, even if that place (like your friend) is gone. So, even when the outer function has finished running, the inner function can still use the variables from that outer function. It's like having a secret stash of snacks that never goes away!
